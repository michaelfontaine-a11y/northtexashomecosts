## What moves the price

- Scope and finish tier — DFW landscaping runs $8–$15 per sq ft for basic work, $15–$30 for mid-range design, and $30–$60+ for high-end custom installs
- North Texas black clay soil — grading and soil amendment add $500–$1,500 to most sod projects, and drainage engineering is often 15–30% of a Dallas project's total (the most underestimated line item)
- Irrigation zone count and water pressure — zones cost $800–$1,500 each, and two same-size yards can quote $10,000 vs $20,000+ depending on water pressure and meter size
- Design fees — $300–$1,500 per site plan or $50–$150/hour for a landscape designer; 3D renderings add another $400–$500
- Plant and material selection — Bermuda sod material is $0.35–$0.70/sq ft vs $0.50–$0.85 for Zoysia, and a mature planted tree ($890–$2,700) costs several times a smaller one ($400+)
- Drainage needs — standalone drainage solutions for clay-heavy Dallas lots run $3,000–$12,000
