## What moves the price

- Door size — a two-car door roughly doubles material cost: $1,093–$1,674 for a one-car vs $2,186–$3,348 for a two-car door installed (Homewyse data via This Old House, 2026)
- Material — installed national ranges run steel $1,093–$1,288, wood $1,674–$1,869, and glass $1,800–$6,070 (This Old House/Homewyse 2026); Angi's Dallas page notes carriage-style doors reach $2,500+ and glass roll-ups exceed $4,000 locally
- Insulation — a basic non-insulated single steel door runs about $600–$900 installed vs $800–$1,200 insulated (HomeGuide 2026), an extra $200–$300 that matters in 100-degree North Texas summers
- Opener replacement — adding or replacing an opener costs $224–$550 in Dallas (average $387, Angi 2026), and heavier wood or glass doors may force a $300+ heavy-duty opener upgrade
- Labor, permits, and tax — Dallas installation labor runs $300–$650, city permits add $50–$200, and the 8.25% Dallas sales tax applies to materials (Angi Dallas 2026)
- Springs and hardware — Dallas heat shortens torsion spring life to roughly 4–6 years vs the typical 7–10, so a standard $155–$361 spring swap or an $825–$1,300 high-cycle upgrade often rides along with a door project (Angi Dallas, Apr 2026)
